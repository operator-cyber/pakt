## The Core Principle: Value-Based Pricing

Don't price against other apps. Price against what Pakt replaces.

### What Pakt Replaces

| Alternative | Cost | Time | What's Missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hire a lawyer | $200-500 per NDA ($460 avg flat fee, $200-350/hr) | 1-2 weeks | Nothing — full service |
| LegalZoom | $59/document or $99/month | Same day | No biometric verification, no video, no state-adaptive language |
| Rocket Lawyer | $39.99/month | Same day | Template-based, no mobile-first signing flow |
| Upwork/Fiverr lawyer | $75-300 | 2-5 days | Variable quality, no app experience |
| Free template (Google) | $0 | 30 min | No compliance, no verification, no legal enforceability features |

**Pakt's position:** Mobile-first, instant, 50-state compliant, biometric verified, video recorded. More than templates, faster than lawyers, cheaper than both.

## App Store Pricing Psychology

### What the Data Shows
- **Charm pricing (.99)** outperforms round numbers by **24%**
- $4.99 feels like $4; $9.99 feels like $9 (left-digit effect)
- Moving from $4.99 to $5.00 = **4.5% drop in sales** (avoid round numbers)
- **$9.99-$19.99** is the sweet spot for professional utility apps
- A tool at $4.99 signals "toy." At $9.99+ it signals "professional"

### The Decoy Effect
Three tiers, most people pick the middle:
```
Basic:   $4.99  (limited)     ← makes Pro look reasonable
Pro:     $9.99  (full app)    ← most people choose this
Premium: $19.99 (everything)  ← makes Pro look like a deal
```

### Price Anchoring for Legal Apps
Frame the price against lawyer fees, not against other apps:
- "Professional NDAs in 5 minutes. The $9.99 alternative to $500 legal fees."
- "Traditional route: Hire lawyer ($200-500+), wait 1-2 weeks. Pakt: $9.99, ready in 5 minutes."

## How to Test and Find the Optimal Price

### Method 1: Launch and A/B Test (Recommended)
1. Launch at $9.99
2. After 60 days, check conversion rate
3. Above 2% conversion → probably underpriced, test $14.99
4. Below 1% conversion → test $6.99
5. Use **RevenueCat** (free under $2,500/month revenue) for A/B price testing on App Store

### Method 2: Van Westendorp Survey (Pre-Launch)
Ask 50-100 potential users 4 questions:
1. At what price would this seem too cheap to trust?
2. At what price would this seem like a bargain?
3. At what price would this feel acceptable?
4. At what price would this be too expensive?

Run via Google Form or Conjointly (free tier). Post in:
- Reddit: r/freelance, r/smallbusiness, r/Entrepreneur
- LinkedIn freelancer groups
- IndieHackers community

Where answers cluster = your price point.

### Method 3: Landing Page Test ($100-200)
1. Create simple landing page describing Pakt
2. Run $100-200 in targeted ads to freelancers/creators
3. Test different price points on different ad variants
4. Measure click-through on "Buy" button at each price
5. Highest revenue (clicks x price) wins

| $4.99 | $4.24 | $5,300 |

## Recommended Launch Price

### $9.99 One-Time Purchase

**Why:**
- 98% cheaper than a lawyer ($460)
- 83-90% cheaper than LegalZoom ($59-99/document)
- Signals "professional tool" not "toy"
- NDA Blue charges $19-25/NDA with worse features — validates $9.99+
- Celebrity NDA charges $50-1000 with 4.7 stars and fewer features

### Future Expansion (Month 6-9)
Add subscription tier for power users:
- $4.99/month or $49.99/year for unlimited NDAs + premium features
- Keeps one-time $9.99 option for casual users
- Captures both segments

**Don't say:** "NDA generator app for $9.99"

**Do say:** "Professional NDAs in 5 minutes. The $9.99 alternative to $500 legal fees. 50-state compliant. Biometric verified. Legally robust."

The first sentence anchors against the lawyer price. Everything after justifies why $9.99 is a steal.

## Post-Launch Monitoring

Track these at 30, 60, and 90 days:
- **Conversion rate** (impressions to downloads): Below 1% = reposition or lower price
- **Revenue per impression**: The real metric — high price x low conversion can beat low price x high conversion
- **Reviews mentioning price**: "Great value" = could charge more. "Too expensive" = reposition messaging, not necessarily price
- **Regional performance**: Different countries may need different price tiers
